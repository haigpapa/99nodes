### Longer version (for a full about page, ~200 words):

```
Haig Papazian doesn't work in disciplines. He works in systems.

The training is architectural — a Bachelor's from AUB, a
Master's in Architectural History from the Bartlett at UCL.
But the thesis was always about erasure: how cities encode
power, how digital images degrade memory, how the buildings
that get demolished tell the truest stories.

That lens carried into music. As co-founder and violinist
of Mashrou' Leila, the work was never just a band — it was
a counter-public, a platform for queer Arab identity at a
time when visibility was a political act. From Baalbeck to
the Hollywood Bowl. Until a concert in Cairo in 2017 became
something else entirely.

In exile in New York, the practice shifted inward. Solo
projects — the Space Time Tuning Machine, the Harmonic Field
Project, the Localization Gap Audit — apply architectural
rigor to music, code, and AI research. The thread running
through all of it: what gets preserved, what gets erased,
and what can be rebuilt from the fragments.

This portfolio is an attempt to map that work. 99 nodes.
14 years. Non-linear by design.
```

## VOICE SUMMARY

The portfolio voice has three registers:

1. **The brief** — one-sentence project descriptions that read like architectural program notes. Precise nouns, active verbs, no adjectives unless the adjective is load-bearing. *"The unplayed string as a measure of distance from home."*

2. **The frame** — nav labels, UI copy, hints. All lowercase. One word where possible. Functional without being sterile.

3. **The context** — the about panel, longer project descriptions. Here the voice opens up. Still precise, but allows the personal and the political to surface. Sentences that start with a fact and land on an implication.

**What to avoid:**
- "award-winning", "critically acclaimed", "renowned"
- Passive voice in descriptions ("was created", "was performed")
- Over-explaining the concept before the work can speak
- Exclamation marks anywhere
- "innovative", "cutting-edge", "pioneering"
- Starting sentences with "I" in third-person contexts
- "multidisciplinary" as a standalone identifier — it's the category, not the practice
